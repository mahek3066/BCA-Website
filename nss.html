Empowering Youth, Serving Nation

Introduction

The National Service Scheme (NSS) is one of the most prominent youth organizations in India, established with the aim of developing the personality of student youth through community service. Launched on 24th September 1969 by the Ministry of Education, Government of India, this scheme was introduced to commemorate the birth centenary of Mahatma Gandhi.

Guided by the motto "Not Me, But You", NSS embodies the spirit of selfless service and encourages students to prioritize the needs of the community over their personal interests. The scheme has grown to become one of the largest student volunteer organizations in the world.

History

When it was first launched on 24th September 1969, the NSS began with a modest beginning of 37 universities and approximately 40,000 student volunteers. The scheme was inspired by the ideals of Mahatma Gandhi and aimed to establish a meaningful linkage between education and social service.

Over the past five decades, the NSS has grown exponentially and now stands as one of the largest youth organizations in the world. Today, it covers over 900+ universities, 18,000+ colleges, and has mobilized more than 38 lakh (3.8 million) student volunteers across India.

The first NSS unit was started at 37 universities with 40,000 volunteers in 1969. Since then, the scheme has expanded to cover all states and union territories of India, making it a truly national movement for youth engagement in social service.

Objectives of NSS

1

Personality Development

Through community service, students develop their overall personality including communication skills, confidence, and self-esteem.

2

Social Responsibility

Instilling social responsibility and civic consciousness among students to understand and address community problems.

3

National Integration

Promoting national integration and communal harmony by bringing together students from different backgrounds.

4

Leadership Development

Cultivating leadership and organizational skills through planning and executing various community service activities.

5

Environmental Awareness

Creating environmental awareness and promoting conservation of natural resources through tree plantation and cleanliness drives.

6

Disaster Preparedness

Training students in disaster preparedness and response to help during natural calamities and emergencies.

7

Moral and Ethical Values

Imparting moral and ethical values to students to become responsible citizens with strong character.

8

Community Service

Providing opportunities for community service and encouraging students to actively participate in social welfare programs.

Our Activities

NSS organizes various activities throughout the year to serve the community and develop students. These activities are broadly categorized into regular activities and special camping programs.

  • Blood Donation Camps - Organizing voluntary blood donation drives to save lives
  • Tree Plantation Drives - Planting trees for environmental conservation and green India
  • Health Awareness Campaigns - Creating awareness about health, hygiene, and disease prevention
  • Literacy Programs - Teaching underprivileged children and adults to read and write
  • Clean-up Drives (Swachh Bharat) - Promoting cleanliness and sanitation in public places
  • Disaster Relief Activities - Helping during natural calamities and emergencies
